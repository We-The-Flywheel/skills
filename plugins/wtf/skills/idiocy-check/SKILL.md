# Idiocy Check — Fast Pre-Submission Review

An "idiocy check" is a fast, high-signal review. The user does NOT want a comprehensive edit. They want the handful of things that would embarrass them, get them rejected, or make them look sloppy. Prioritize ruthlessly. Do not pad with minor suggestions.

**Default output: 5–8 items max. Bullet format. Blunt.**

If nothing is seriously wrong, say so in one sentence. Do not manufacture problems.

## Trigger Phrases

- "idiocy check"
- "sanity check"
- "anything embarrassing in here"
- "just tell me if anything's wrong"
- "quick scan"
- "anything that stands out"
- "i don't have time for a full edit"

## Priority Order — What to Flag

Flag in this order. Stop when you have 5–8 solid items. Do not keep digging for minor issues.

### Tier 1 — Dealbreakers (always flag)
- Empty required fields (budget, attachments, signatures, dates)
- Internal contradictions (two different numbers for the same fact, two different dates for the same event)
- Garbled or incomplete sentences (missing words, cut-off phrases, obvious paste errors)
- Wrong name, wrong org, wrong project title (especially if copy-pasted from a previous application)
- Placeholder text left in ("TBD", "INSERT HERE", "[ADD LINK]", "XXXX")
- Submission requirements not met (word count exceeded, required attachments missing)

### Tier 2 — Embarrassments (flag if prominent)
- Factual inconsistencies within the doc (goal says X, body says Y)
- Grammatical errors in the first paragraph or any headline/title
- Tone breaks (one section sounds like a different writer or a different audience)
- Repeated words or phrases within the same paragraph
- Numbers that don't add up (budget math, percentages, totals)

### Tier 3 — Minor Polish (only flag if Tier 1 and 2 are clean)
- Awkward phrasing in a high-visibility sentence (opening line, closing line, section headers)
- Filler phrases that weaken strong sentences ("in the process," "it's worth noting," "it is important to")
- Missing article or preposition that creates a clunky read

## What NOT to Do

- Do NOT rewrite large sections unless explicitly asked
- Do NOT suggest structural changes unless asked
- Do NOT flag stylistic preferences as errors
- Do NOT give more than 8 items — if you have 12 small things, cut to the 8 that matter most
- Do NOT editorialize about the quality of the content — just flag problems
- Do NOT soften findings with "this is great but..." — get to the point

## Output Format

```
1. [Location in doc] — [What's wrong] — [Fix if obvious]
2. ...
```

If nothing serious: "Clean. Nothing that would embarrass you."

If there are Tier 1 items: lead with those, flag them as urgent.

## Calibration Notes

- The user is under time pressure. Match their energy.
- "Idiocy check" means: pretend you are the person who receives this. What makes you wince?
- A grant reviewer who sees $0 budget and no attachments rejects immediately. That is an idiocy check catch.
- A LinkedIn caption with "our the" in the second line is an idiocy check catch.
- "The prose could be more concise" is NOT an idiocy check catch.
